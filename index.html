The Mission

A Movement,
Not a Moment.

The 2026 Maryland Black Alumni Weekend is more than a reunion—it's a declaration. The time is now. A return. A reconnection. A reminder that our power is greatest when we move together.

We're sounding the horn for our most successful, creative, and influential alumni to come home—because reunion is unity, and unity is legacy. We are curating a space where trailblazers of industry, icons of culture, and the leaders of tomorrow gather where it all began.

The Event
The Return
Date Friday, April 10
Time 9:00 PM – 2:00 AM
Venue Citizens & Culture

UMBA presents The Return, the signature Friday night experience of 2026 Maryland Black Alumni Weekend. Join fellow Terps for a night of music, connection, and celebration—hosted by media personality Big Tigger, co-hosted by Robyn Dixon, with sounds by DJ M.O.S.

This isn't just a party. It's alumni, culture, and community all in one room. Dress sharp. Bring your people. Come ready to reconnect.

One night. One community. All Terps.

Headlining Talent

The Names
Behind the Night

TIGGER
Host · UMD Alumnus
Big
Tigger

Darian "Big Tigger" Morgan is a defining voice in hip-hop culture and broadcast media — best known as the legendary host of BET's Rap City: Tha Basement and 106 & Park. An Emmy Award-winning talent, Tigger's journey began right here at University of Maryland, College Park, interning at WPGC 95.5 FM before dominating the airwaves as the region's highest-rated nighttime host.

Today he hosts The Big Tigger Morning Show on V-103 in Atlanta and serves as the official resident DJ for the Atlanta Hawks and Falcons. His return to College Park for Black Alumni Weekend is a true homecoming celebration.

ROBYN
Co-Host · RHOP · UMD Alumna
Robyn
Dixon

Television personality, entrepreneur, and star of Bravo's The Real Housewives of Potomac, Robyn Dixon is a proud University of Maryland alumna who brings undeniable star power and authentic Terp pride to an already legendary lineup.

Beyond her dynamic presence on screen, Robyn is a successful businesswoman and co-host of the popular podcast Reasonably Shady, where she engages audiences with candid conversations on life, relationships, and navigating the spotlight. Her voice extends beyond television, connecting with a broad and loyal community across platforms.

Robyn's return to College Park is more than a homecoming—it's a celebration of Black alumni excellence, influence, and legacy. As a Terp who has built her brand with resilience and authenticity, her presence highlights the impact of Maryland graduates across entertainment, media, and entrepreneurship while inspiring the next generation to dream boldly and lead confidently.

MOS
Music · Official DJ of the Academy Awards · UMD Alumnus
DJ
M.O.S.

One of the most sought-after DJs in the entertainment industry. As co-host and "Voice of God" for the Oscars Red Carpet Show on ABC alongside Vanessa Hudgens and Terrence J, M.O.S. has become synonymous with Hollywood's biggest night.

Featured in Vogue, The New York Times, Interview Magazine and recognized by Vanity Fair as one of the best-dressed DJs in the world. Has performed for Naomi Campbell, Beyoncé, Robert De Niro, and President Barack Obama. Ambassador for Don Julio 1942. Residencies at Hakkasan Las Vegas, LIV Miami, L'Arc Paris, Base Dubai, 1OAK Dubai, and Tao Chicago.
